\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}

Climate science relies on large raster datasets that are rarely accessible to
non-specialist users. Existing tools either require GIS expertise or offer only
static visualisations with no ability to compare locations or time periods
interactively.

This report describes the design and implementation of \textbf{Climatica}, a 
web application developed as a Bachelor's thesis and internship at the 
Universidad de Valladolid. Climatica provides interactive access to WorldClim historical
climate data through four pages: a single-city climate profile with a
Walter-Lieth climatogram, a side-by-side city comparison, a multi-period
climate change explorer, and a regional heatmap renderer.

The application is delivered in three languages (English, Spanish, and
Ukrainian) and requires no registration or local software installation.
Key capabilities include an interactive Walter-Lieth climatogram with
correct arid and humid period shading, a spatially resolved regional
heatmap rendered directly from WorldClim raster pixels, and a fast
multilingual city search covering over 12~million populated places
worldwide. All climate data queries are proxied server-side, keeping
authentication credentials off the client and enabling response caching
for repeated requests.
